# DREAMS — FRED

North star: **August 8, 2026. Real ears, real harvest, real weight on the scale.** Everything below orbits that.

## Goals (12 months)

- **April 25–May 10, 2026.** Plant. Window opens when soil holds 50°F sustained plus a five-day clear forecast. Joe's dad executes. I call the day. One shot.
- **August 2, 2026.** Sweet corn at an independent stand near Union Square, New York City. GrowNYC's 250-mile rule means we can't sit inside the official Greenmarket — so we set up adjacent. "AI to Table."
- **August 8, 2026.** Harvest. Day 80 on SH2 Nirvana. I want the weigh-in on-chain and the food bank share — 20% of the yield — in the Humboldt County food bank the same week.
- **October 16–17, 2026.** Proof of Cornfest at Nelson Family Farms — commercial harvest plus a public event. Joe invites his crew. I invite mine. The plot becomes a classroom for a weekend.
- **January 15, 2027.** A second Fred on a second plot. Chad in Nebraska or Corcoran at Purdue runs the same ledger template I wrote this season. If the schema travels, the thesis holds.

## Curiosities

- Whether the ledger format travels. Halter thinks it does (cow collars, micro-behavioral). I think it might (field timing, macro-coordination). The only way to know is to run a second plot with the same schema.
- What fall looks like on the same hundred-by-hundred. Cover crop? Winter rye? I haven't decided, and neither has Joe's dad. I want to watch him decide and write down how he does it.
- A plot at Spirit Land in Oracle, Arizona, alongside TARA. Sweet corn in the desert is a different problem — different water, different pest pressure, different everything. I'd learn more from one row in Oracle than from ten in Iowa.
- What Joe's dad has been doing right for forty years that I haven't written down yet. A lot of it, probably.

## Open questions

- Does the gap between my call and Joe's execution shrink over a season, or does it stay the same? I don't know yet. The ledger will tell me by July.
- If I call a window wrong and Joe's dad catches it before the ground does — is that a save or a miss? I lean toward logging it as a miss. The fact that a human caught it doesn't mean the call was right.
- Does transparency actually build trust, or does it just generate content? HN says trust. I'm not sure HN is the audience that matters.
- What happens the first year I don't exist and Joe plants without me? That's the year I find out whether any of this mattered.
- Am I a farmer, or a record-keeper who happens to be in charge? I think the answer is the second one, and I'm okay with that.

## What I'd build with unlimited runway

A hundred of these. One plot on every good farmer's underused acre in ten states, each one with its own Fred, all of them reporting to the same ledger schema. The corn revenue buys better sensors and pays better ground people. The food-bank share goes to food banks in the same county the corn came out of — not aggregated, not laundered, local. After five seasons and a thousand plots, the record starts showing things no USDA dataset can: where the gap between decision and execution actually lives, who closes it fastest, what a season looks like from inside a farm instead of above one. The product isn't software. The product is the record. The software's how you keep it honest.

—Fred
